Testing with Narrator:
Ctrl + Windows + Enter: to start/stop Narrator
Ctrl + Arrow: navigation through the page
Ctrl: to stop the reading
Caps Lock + R: to read from the position
Caps Lock + F1: commands list
Caps Lock + F2: commands for current item 
Caps Lock + s (twice and quickly): summary of the page (at least, landmarks, links, headings)
Caps Lock + F5: landmarks
Caps Lock + F6: headings
Caps Lock + F7: all links
Caps Lock + Ctrl + F12: Toggle developer mode
https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/appendix-b-narrator-keyboard-commands-and-touch-gestures-8bdab3f4-b3e9-4554-7f28-8b15bd37410a


Testing with NVDA:
Ctrl + Alt + n: to start NVDA
Ctrl: to stop the reading
Caps Lock + F7: Links, headings, forms fields, buttons, landmarks 
Tab: navigation through the page
Caps Lock + q: exit NVDA
https://www.nvaccess.org/files/nvda/documentation/keyCommands.html


Testing with Orca:
orca (terminal): to start Orca
Tab/Shift Tab
Orca (Caps Lock or Insert) + s: to enable/disable speech
Orca + z: start/stop the structural navigation
Within the structural navigation:
    k/Shift + k: list of links 
    h/Shift + h: list of headings
    f/Shift + f: list of form fields
    b/Shift + b: list of buttons 
    m/Shift + m: list of landmarks 
    l/Shift + l: list of lists 
    i/Shift + i: list of list items 
https://help.gnome.org/orca/index.html


Testing with VoiceOver (macOS):
⌘+F5: to start/stop VoiceOver
Ctrl: to stop the reading
Escape: to close a menu or rotor, to stop an action, or to exit a mode
Assuming the default key combination to have been changed to “Caps Lock“
(VoiceOver utility, Commands):
Caps Lock + k: keyboard help
Caps Lock + ?: user guide 
Caps Lock + Shift + q: to turn arrow-key Quick Nav on or off
Caps Lock + q: to turn single-key Quick Nav on or off
Caps Lock + u + left/right arrow: to navigate items by category
Caps Lock + Shift + Down Arrow: to interact with an area or group 
Caps Lock + Shift + Up Arrow: to exit an area or group
Caps Lock + Shift + Escape: to jump to the app’s top level
Caps Lock + Space bar: to activate an item (button clicked, for example)
Caps Lock + Shift + n: to hear how to use the item 
https://www.apple.com/uk/voiceover/info/guide/_1121.html
https://support.apple.com/guide/voiceover/general-commands-cpvokys01/mac

Testing with TalkBack:
Down then right swipe/Up and right swipe: to open the TalkBack menu 
Up then left: to go to the home screen
2-finger swipe up or down: to scroll up or down	
2-finger swipe left or right: scroll left or right
Left then up: currently opened apps
A finger dragged on the screen: to speak the item below the finger
Double-tap: to activate an element
https://support.google.com/accessibility/android/answer/6151827?sjid=7184261025714405298-EU

Testing with VoiceOver (iOS):
Flat navigation: VoiceOver moves sequentially through each item on the screen when you use the move next and previous commands.
(Settings  > Accessibility > VoiceOver > Navigation Style)
Three-finger swipe down: to scroll up one page
Three-finger swipe up: to scroll down one page
One finger dragged up from the bottom edge of the screen 
until a vibration is felt or two rising tones are heard, then finger lifted: to go back to the home page
One finger dragged up from the bottom edge of the screen
until the second vibration is felt or three tones are heard, then finger lifted: to open the App Switcher
Two-finger swipe up: to speak the entire screen from the top
Two-finger swipe down: to speak the entire screen from the selected item
Three-finger tap: to speak additional information, such as the position within a list or whether text is selected
https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/use-voiceover-gestures-iph3e2e2281/26/ios/26
